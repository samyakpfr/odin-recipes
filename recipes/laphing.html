Laphing

Laphing is a spicy and delicious food that looks like thick noodles.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup mung bean flour (or all-purpose flour, for alternative method)
  • 3 cups water (for batter)
  • 2â€“3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp vinegar
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1/2 tsp chili oil (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional: Sichuan pepper powder, green onion, coriander for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Mix mung bean flour and water until smooth. Let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Cook the mixture over medium heat, stirring continuously until it thickens and becomes glossy.
  3. Pour the cooked mixture into a flat tray to cool. Let it set completely (about 1 hour).
  4. Once set, cut the jelly-like laphing into strips or desired shapes.
  5. In a bowl, mix garlic, soy sauce, vinegar, sesame oil, chili oil, and salt to make the sauce.
  6. Toss the laphing strips in the sauce and garnish with green onion, coriander, or Sichuan pepper if desired.
  7. Serve cold as a spicy, savory snack.
